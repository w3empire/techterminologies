What Does 'PPVPN (Provider-Provisioned VPN)' Mean?

author Adam Sabin

A Provider-Provisioned VPN (PPVPN) is a type of virtual private network (VPN) that is provided and maintained by a service provider. It gives customers the ability to connect to the provider’s network over the internet and access resources or services as if they were connected directly to the provider’s network.

PPVPNs are often used by businesses to connect remote offices and employees to the company’s main network or by individuals to securely access the internet from public Wi-Fi hotspots. PPVPNs can be implemented using a variety of technologies, such as MPLS, L2TP, and OpenVPN.

Categories

PPVPNs can be divided into two main categories: layer 2 and layer 3.

A Layer 2 PPVPN, also known as a “Virtual Private LAN Service” (VPLS), uses Multi-Protocol Label Switching (MPLS) to create a virtual private LAN across a public infrastructure. This allows for the exchange of broadcast and multicast traffic and provides support for VLAN tagging. This category of PPVPN is often used for interconnecting multiple LANs in different locations.

A Layer 3 PPVPN, also known as a “Virtual Private Routed Network” (VPRN), uses a variety of routing protocols to create a virtual private routed network across a public infrastructure. This makes possible the exchange of unicast traffic and support for IP address allocation. This variation of PPVPN is often used for connecting remote offices and employees to the company’s main network.

Advantages

PPVPNs have many advantages over traditional VPNs, such as dedicated VPN gateways, scalability, and reliability. PPVPNs also have the capacity to support Quality of Service (QoS) and traffic engineering. It is also easier to manage because the service provider manages the VPN infrastructure, thus minimizing the need for customer intervention.

PPVPNs are a service that is commonly provided by many internet service providers and telecom companies, and they can be provided to customers as a managed service, with the provider handling the setup, configuration, and maintenance of the VPN.

FAQ

What are the different types of PPVPNs?

The different types of PPVPNs include Virtual Private LAN Service (VPLS), Layer 2 Tunneling Protocol (L2TP) over Internet Protocol Security (IPsec), and Multiprotocol Label Switching (MPLS) VPNs.

How does a PPVPN compare to other types of VPNs, such as customer-provisioned VPNs?

Compared to customer-provisioned VPNs, PPVPNs are usually managed and maintained by a service provider, allowing for easier scaling and management. PPVPNs can also offer more advanced features, such as Quality of Service (QoS) and traffic engineering.

How does a PPVPN work, and what are the underlying technologies used?

A PPVPN works by creating a secure connection between multiple locations, such as remote offices, over a public network. The underlying technologies used include VPLS, L2TP over IPsec, and MPLS.

What are the benefits of using a PPVPN, such as scalability and reliability?

Using a PPVPN has benefits like scalability because the service provider can manage and maintain the network, and reliability because they usually have multiple backups in place to make sure the network is always up.

How is a PPVPN typically implemented and managed?

A PPVPN is normally implemented and managed by a service provider. The provider will handle the setup, configuration, and ongoing maintenance of the VPN.

What are the typical use cases for a PPVPN, such as connecting remote offices or securely accessing the internet from public Wi-Fi hotspots?

PPVPNs are commonly used to connect remote offices, securely access the internet from public Wi-Fi hotspots, and provide secure connections for mobile employees.

What are the security considerations for a PPVPN, and how are they addressed by service providers?

Security considerations for a PPVPN include ensuring the confidentiality and integrity of data transmitted over the VPN, as well as protecting against unauthorized access. Service providers generally address these concerns through the use of encryption and secure authentication methods.

How does a PPVPN support Quality of Service (QoS) and traffic engineering?

PPVPNs can support QoS and traffic engineering by prioritizing certain types of traffic, such as voice or video, over others. This ensures that critical applications receive the bandwidth they need.

What are the costs associated with a PPVPN, and how does it compare to other VPN solutions?

The cost of a PPVPN will vary depending on the service provider and the specific service plan. However, PPVPNs can be more expensive than other VPN solutions, such as customer-provisioned VPNs.

How does a PPVPN support load balancing, failover, and disaster recovery?

PPVPNs can support load balancing, failover, and disaster recovery by providing multiple connections to different locations and by using redundant network infrastructure. If one connection or network component fails, traffic can be routed through a different path.

Conclusion

In summary, PPVPN is a flexible and cost-effective solution for businesses and organizations that need to securely connect multiple remote locations and users to their main network over the internet. It is also a good solution for individuals who want to securely access the internet from public Wi-Fi hotspots.
